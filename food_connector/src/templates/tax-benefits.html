Tax Benefits of Food Donation in South Africa

Donating surplus food or other resources can help your organization save on tax while addressing hunger and supporting vulnerable communities. In South Africa, Section 18A of the Income Tax Act allows donors to receive tax deductions for qualifying donations made to registered Public Benefit Organizations (PBOs).

Eligibility for Tax Deductions

To qualify for a tax deduction, the following conditions must be met:

  • The donation must be made to a PBO or a qualifying institution approved by SARS.
  • The donation must be in cash or kind (e.g., food or other goods).
  • The PBO must issue a valid Section 18A certificate to the donor.

Donors can claim up to 10% of their taxable income for qualifying donations, helping to offset tax liabilities while contributing to social good.

Additional Benefits for Companies

Beyond individual tax benefits, companies that donate food or other resources can also reduce their taxable income. Food donations align with Corporate Social Responsibility (CSR) initiatives, enhancing brand reputation and fostering goodwill within communities.

Companies that donate food can also benefit from reduced waste disposal costs, contributing to both environmental sustainability and financial savings.
