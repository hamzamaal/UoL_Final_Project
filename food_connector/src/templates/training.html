Food Safety and Training

Learn how to safely donate and distribute surplus food.

Ensuring Food Safety

At Food Connector, we prioritize the safety of the food we help redistribute. Whether you are a donor or a recipient, understanding and following proper food safety guidelines is essential.

Why Food Safety Matters

Safe food handling is critical to ensure that surplus food remains healthy and safe for consumption. Our training resources provide guidance to donors and recipients on how to maintain the quality of food throughout the donation process.

Training Programs

We offer comprehensive food safety and handling training tailored to food donors, community organizations, and volunteers.

  • Food Storage: Proper storage temperatures and handling techniques to prevent spoilage.
  • Labeling and Tracking: Ensuring donated food is properly labeled and traceable.
  • Transportation Safety: Guidelines for safely transporting perishable and non-perishable goods.

Guidelines for Donors

If you’re donating food, here are some basic guidelines to follow:

  • Ensure that all food is within its “use by” or “best before” date.
  • Store food at appropriate temperatures until it is delivered or picked up.
  • Clearly label all food donations with contents and dates.
  • Follow local health department regulations for prepared and perishable foods.

Training for Recipients

Recipients of food donations can also benefit from our safety training programs. Learn how to:

  • Inspect and safely accept donated food.
  • Store perishable food to prevent spoilage.
  • Distribute food to communities while maintaining health and safety standards.

Get Started

Ready to learn more about food safety? Contact us for more information on our training programs and resources tailored to your organization’s needs.
